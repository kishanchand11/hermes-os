# Part I – Executive Constitution

# 04. Scope

## 4.1 Purpose

This document defines the boundaries of Hermes OS development and establishes the initial operational scope of the Base DEX arbitrage project.

Scope control prevents uncontrolled expansion and ensures engineering resources remain focused on the primary objective.

---

## 4.2 Initial System Scope

Hermes OS will initially build a decentralized exchange arbitrage system operating on:

- Base blockchain.
- Decentralized exchanges.
- Local development environments.

The system is designed for engineering validation, research, simulation, and controlled execution workflows.

---

## 4.3 Blockchain Scope

The initial supported blockchain environment is Base only.

Other blockchain networks are outside the current scope unless approved through governance and documented through an Architecture Decision Record.

---

## 4.4 Exchange Scope

The system focuses exclusively on decentralized exchange infrastructure.

Centralized exchange integrations are excluded from the initial architecture.

---

## 4.5 Deployment Scope

The initial deployment target is localhost.

Local-first development enables:

- Reproducible environments.
- Controlled testing.
- Faster iteration.
- Safer experimentation.

---

## 4.6 Engineering Scope

The project includes:

- Architecture design.
- DevOps infrastructure.
- Automated testing.
- Simulation systems.
- Backtesting systems.
- Arbitrage engine development.
- Monitoring and operations.
- Knowledge management.

---

## 4.7 Out of Scope

The following are excluded unless formally approved:

- Multi-chain expansion.
- Centralized exchanges.
- Unvalidated automation.
- Guaranteed profit claims.
- Production deployment without validation.

---

## 4.8 Scope Change Process

Any scope change requires:

1. Written justification.
2. Impact analysis.
3. Architecture review.
4. Governance approval.
5. Documentation update.

Scope changes must preserve the project's engineering principles.
